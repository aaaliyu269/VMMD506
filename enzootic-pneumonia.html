Enzootic Pneumonia of Calves

Enzootic pneumonia is a multifactorial respiratory disease of young calves, also known as Calf Pneumonia Complex. It occurs in intensively managed dairy or beef herds and is most common in calves less than 6 months of age.

Key Characteristics

Age Affected

Calves less than 6 months

Seasonality

Cool or damp weather

Economic Impact

Mortality, reduced weight gain, treatment costs

Etiology

Causative Agents

  • Bacterial contributors:
    • Pasteurella multocida
    • Mannheimia haemolytica
    • Mycoplasma bovis
    • Histophilus somni
  • Viral contributors:
    • Bovine herpesvirus 1
    • Bovine coronavirus
Epidemiology
  • Occurs worldwide
  • More common in housed calves with poor ventilation
  • Predisposing factors:
    • Poor ventilation
    • Overcrowding
    • Sudden temperature changes
    • Inadequate colostrum intake
    • Transportation stress
    • Poor hygiene
Pathogenesis
  • Stress predisposes calves to viral infections
  • Virus has affinity for respiratory epithelial cells
  • Viral replication damages epithelial lining of airways
  • Secondary bacterial invasion through damaged tissues
  • Inflammatory response increases mucus production
  • Bronchopneumonia and lung consolidation occur
  • Impaired gas exchange leads to respiratory distress
Clinical Signs
  • Nasal discharge (serous to mucopurulent)
  • Coughing
  • Fever (39.5°C - 41.5°C)
  • Harsh lung sounds
  • Weight loss
  • Depression
  • Anorexia
  • Increased respiratory rate
  • Extended head and neck
Diagnosis
  • History and clinical signs
  • Auscultation for abnormal lung sounds
  • Blood test for fibrinogen levels
  • Thoracic radiography
  • Tracheal wash for culture and sensitivity
  • Postmortem examination
Differential Diagnosis
  • Acute pasteurellosis - sudden onset, high fever, severe pneumonia
  • Bovine respiratory disease complex - similar presentation but can affect older cattle
  • Aspiration pneumonia - history of inhaling feed or liquids, localized lung changes
  • Infectious Bovine Rhinotracheitis - acute dyspnea and respiratory signs and abortion
  • Lungworm infection - parasitic cause, different treatment

Treatment

  • Antimicrobials (e.g., tetracyclines, florfenicol)
  • Anti-inflammatories (e.g., flunixin)
  • Supportive care (fluids, soft feeds)
  • Isolation of affected calves
  • Bronchodilators if needed
  • Expectorants to help clear airways

Prevention and Control

Colostrum Management

Ensure adequate colostrum intake in newborn calves

Ventilation

Maintain good ventilation in housing

Housing

Avoid overcrowding

Hygiene

Routine cleaning and disinfection of pens

Vaccination

Consider vaccination against key pathogens

Stress Reduction

Minimize handling and transportation stress

Important Note

Early recognition and treatment significantly improves outcomes. Calves that recover may have reduced growth rates and permanent lung damage affecting future productivity.

Economic Impact

Enzootic pneumonia causes significant economic losses due to treatment costs, reduced growth rates, increased susceptibility to other diseases, and in severe cases, mortality.
